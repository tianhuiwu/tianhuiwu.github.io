Greetings! I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at the University of Iowa. I received my MA in Political Theory from Sun Yat-sen University and my BA in Political Science and Public Administration from Fudan University. My research interests lie in comparative public opinion and political behavior. I employ both statistical and computational methods to study perceived corruption, mass media, and electoral politics. In my dissertation, I explore how state-run media and propaganda shape individuals' perceptions of corruption in authoritarian regimes.
